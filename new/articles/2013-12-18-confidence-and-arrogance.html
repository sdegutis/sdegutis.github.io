Confidence and Arrogance Sometimes I feel really confident in my abilities. Not over-confident. I don't think I can replace the JVM or OS X any time soon (probably never in my life). Just confident that I can do what I think I can do. And even when I can't do what I think I can do, I try to be honest with myself and with others about _how_ confident I am that I can do something, in percentages. But sometimes I wonder if I'm venturing into the land of arrogance. I'm not really sure what the difference between confidence and arrogance is though. I think it has to do with a sense of inflated importance. I try to remember that nobody is more important than anyone else. To give an extreme example, sure, the President probably won't take my phone call, but it's not because he's more important than me, it's just because he's a busy guy. I try to remember that I'm not more important than anyone else, and my projects aren't more important than theirs. So I think it's possible to be confident in your abilities without being arrogant about them. And I think it's a kind of false-humility to say you're not good at something when you really are. With that said, I'm only kind of good at some things, and really good at others. But aren't we all?
