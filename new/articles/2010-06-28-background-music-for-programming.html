Background Music for Programming I've been listening to the official Final Fantasy 7 soundtrack pretty much exclusively for several months now. (I've accepted the fact that my music habits are atypical.) And I don't see an end in sight. This stuff is perfect as programming background music. And I think I know why. I've actually played very few video games. Super Mario and Super Metroid were the first ones I really played, both of which occupied many idle weeks when I was a kid. But Final Fantasy 7 was a whole different beast. Being 3 discs long, a speedrun for me took probably a solid 40-something hours, _after_ I had already become proficient at the game. This game didn't take weeks to master, it took months, probably even years. My first time playing the game probably took me over 100 hours, even with the guide. While playing this game, you're subjected to background music _the entire time_. If it were anything like the soundtrack of Pirates of the Caribbean, you would get worn out very quickly, as that music is intended to be intense and only listened to for a couple hours total. Thus, FFVII's soundtrack is intentionally [softer](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=oeIefheO3h0) and [gentler](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=cjhHx-yk4HE&feature=related) on our ears and brains (and [hearts](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=1ky-cTSKlKQ)). Especially the tracks where you're doing a lot of uninterrupted storytelling or walking around. That's not to say that the soundtrack is boring. Many of the tracks are intended to be [very](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=sMHkW1xBMGQ&feature=PlayList&p=0E115AD112D31758&playnext%5Ffrom=PL&playnext=1&index=83) [intense](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=t7wJ8pE2qKU) and give a [sense](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TDGBUp25i8U) of [urgency](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=7aAtAyeO%5F6w&feature=PlayList&p=DED53DA472CA53B4&playnext%5Ffrom=PL&playnext=1&index=5) (I try to skip these when I'm programming). Some are are both [fast](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=azgxHn5paTg) and [lighthearted](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LvDuyVp17Pw). Some are [very](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Ho1vZh1wFYA), [very](http://www.google.com/search?hl=en&source=hp&q=those+chosen+by+the+planet&aq=f&aqi=&aql=&oq=&gs%5Frfai=), [very](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=XhC7uunwJE4) [creepy](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=H61dOMsLKhw). Honestly, the tracks I try to listen to mostly during work are [pretty](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vcEGFAaSpzI) [neutral](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=FUkgnLzQcY8) and [calm](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Vlnk5ThilSo). Most of the tracks have their [own](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Jj0F2KfCvhg) [very](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=q85KIhPXrIQ) [distinct](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vwBjCKWaO%5F0&feature=PlayList&p=DED53DA472CA53B4&playnext%5Ffrom=PL&playnext=1&index=8) [aura](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=vn3fWyZtk4E). The soundtrack of FFVII actually fills 4 CDs, where most of the tracks looped twice (they were MIDIs intended to be able to loop indefinitely; obviously some single-use tracks don't make sense as a loop). If you're like me and have gotten sucked into this game as a kid, this soundtrack is essentially a bottled form of a several hundred hour emotional journey. If you haven't, it's still probably worth a listen.
