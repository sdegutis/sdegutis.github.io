---
title: "Ignore the Naysayers"
---

When Sublime Text was first released, a few commenters on [his announcement blog post](http://www.sublimetext.com/blog/articles/anatomy-of-a-next-generation-text-editor) were asking why he would even bother writing a text editor when we could just use emacs. They just lacked vision. Jon saw a real need that they didn't.

When Dropbox was first announced, many people expressed doubts that such a service would really take off. Fortunately, they ignored these naysayers, and I use Dropbox probably every day.

I've heard people using the same arguments about Clojure: either we should use Common Lisp, or this or that. I've heard people claim that Datomic is just another functional database, doomed to die with the rest.

Basically, I've heard a lot of naysaying. Maybe it's just really easy to naysay. And I'm really glad so many people ignored it and made great things. Even if the project doesn't turn out successful, that person _learned_ something, and now they're even smarter than before.
