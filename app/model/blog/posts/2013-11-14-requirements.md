title: "Requirements"
---

I watched Iron Man 3 with my wife and brother the other day. What a fantastic movie! Super cool effects, great actors. And really awesome technology.

Actually, I kept thinking about his technology. Specifically, how his software would have looked compared to ours.

In our world, an OS has to conform to the [Advanced Configuration and Power Interface](http://www.acpi.info/DOWNLOADS/ACPIspec50.pdf)specification. All of it. I highly doubt his OSes did. They probably do something similar but much simpler.

For us, the champion of graphical interfaces has been the [X Window System Protocol](https://www.google.com/url?sa=t&rct=j&q=&esrc=s&source=web&cd=3&ved=0CEAQFjAC&url=http%3A%2F%2Fwww.x.org%2Fdocs%2FXProtocol%2Fproto.pdf&ei=T0OEUoWENcSuyQH17oHYDw&usg=AFQjCNEgshJq98JH4hEqWUJTNnPAr4qkYw&sig2=LESUb1BnNSm9SfKT38bysA&bvm=bv.56343320,d.aWc). I'm betting his GUIs use a much simpler protocol.

I bet he used simple, hard-coded fonts instead of [OpenType](http://www.microsoft.com/typography/otspec/default.htm). He probably chose ASCII over trying to cover all of [Unicode 6.3](http://www.unicode.org/versions/Unicode6.3.0/).

He probably just re-thought his requirements.

He didn't need his software to interface with hardware evolved from the 1980s, so he was free to ditch a lot of legacy requirements.

He didn't need his software to be perfectly compatible with the average desktop computer's other software either, so he could ditch most of the protocols and formats we have.
