---
title: "Trial-time calculations need improvement"
---

Over a month ago, I downloaded [Things.app](http://culturedcode.com/things/) planning to try it out and see if I like the way it works. Practically that same day, [a friend](http://lightheadsw.com/) put out a private beta of his own task-management app for Mac, and I have been beta testing it for him ever since. Thus, I haven't actually been using Things.app, and as such, my todo-list in it is barren.

So 3 or 4 weeks pass, and finally I've decided I'd like to try Things out and see if I prefer that to my friend's beta app. Here comes the tricky part... Things.app offers a 15-day trial before it goes into read-only mode, and I neglected this app on my Desktop for longer than this. Thus, the first day I open it to actually use it, I'm suddenly _completely_ unable to actually test it out and see if I like it. 

After speaking with [an employee](http://twitter.com/macguitar) of [Cultured Code](http://culturedcode.com/) who seemed largely unsympathetic, I decided to blog about this ordeal, since this isn't the first time this sort of thing has happened with me and Mac apps, especially considering all the competitive fields out there ([Flow](http://extendmac.com/flow/) versus [Transmit](http://www.panic.com/transmit/), [Dropbox](https://www.getdropbox.com/home) versus [Papaya](http://lightheadsw.com/papaya/), [Coda](http://www.panic.com/coda/) versus [Espresso](http://macrabbit.com/espresso/), etc.)

See, I don't see this as a problem with one specific app, or one company either. Many apps, including mine, use a the very date you first launch the app as the starting point of its trial, and whether you use the app during that period or not, the app's free ride ends N days after the first day. This needs to be calculated a hell of a lot better. I've been brewing up some ideas, and I'd love to hear your thoughts on this. Surely we can't just keep using this very poor solution... or can we? What do you think?
