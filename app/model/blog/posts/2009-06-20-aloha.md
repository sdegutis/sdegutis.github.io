title: "Aloha!"
---

Some have asked me what Aloha is since I started tweeting about it, and I remembered that I never properly blagged about it.

Well, a few days ago, I decided to take a portion of my social networking app and separate it from the app itself, releasing it as a free utility. This little app has 2 functions: to replace [qwitter.com](http://useqwitter.com/) (which is almost completely broken from my experience) and to replace emails from sites like twitter which notify you when you have new followers.

It works by notifying you via [Growl](http://growl.info/) in a very unobtrusive way, when someone stops or starts following you. Clicking the notification (except on the close button) will take you to the page of that user on that service.

![aloha screenshot](/static/old-blog-files/aloha1.png)

Another difference from those services is that Aloha supports multiple services, including Twitter, Facebook, [Identi.ca](http://Identi.ca), and probably more. So if someone on Facebook stops being your friend, for example, you will get a nice little Growl about it.

Aloha stores its information in a Core Data database per Mac OS X user account, which allows for possibilities in the future such as graphing the data, or for new features such as a "re-display last notification" item.

Despite being a freebie, the app is currently in a very limited beta stage, mainly so that if I made any really dumb mistakes, they can go relatively unnoticed -mwahahaha-.
