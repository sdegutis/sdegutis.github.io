---
title: "Programming Background Music"
---

There are a lot of opinions on what music is best to have on while coding. While some people probably don't care and just throw iTunes into Shuffle mode, others (like me) are constantly on the lookout for the perfect programming soundtrack.

First, let me explain that my purposes for listening to music while coding may be different than anyone else's. My whole reason for it, is specifically to help relax me and keep my mind slightly occupied; I've found that I concentrate a whole lot better when my mind is doing some small menial task in the background. For example, I'll play with a small object, like a puzzle or piece of string. Something very small like this. Otherwise, my mind gets too distracted too easily. This may not be the same with anyone else. Or maybe other people have the same thing but a lot less exaggerated than I do. (Plus, if it gets too quiet, I can hear footsteps or outside sounds, and my mind wanders a lot more easily.) So, I listen to music to keep that small part of my brain busy while the rest of it focuses on solving a problem.

I've found that the best coding-music has a few characteristics:

* Should not contain lyrics. Music with lyrics is distracting. Either you end up thinking about the words, or singing along, but in the end, you can only go for so long listening to people sing while trying to concentrate on solving hard problems. Interestingly, this still leaves the door open for a good deal of genres, such as jazz, classical, game soundtracks, and probably more.
* Should not contain shrill-sounding instruments. For a few months I tried listening to the album "Kind of Blue" by Miles Davis, after hearing it in music class and thinking it was awesome. Turns out that after a couple of hours, those wind instruments actually _bypass_ the ears and skip right to piercing your brain.
* Should not be very heavy or dramatic. On the recommendation of @mattgemmell, I tried to code to the soundtrack of The Pirates of the Caribbean, but ultimately, it physically _exhausted_ me after only a few hours. I can't explain why, I just know I felt like I had run a few miles after listening to that epic (albeit great) music.
* Slower is better. After listening to a few different jazz albums and artists, I realized that if I'm sitting down for a 10-hour coding session, anything too fast just makes it very difficult to concentrate, even if it meets all the previous pre-requisits.

For a while I tried the soundtrack of Final Fantasy 7, but even though it's wonderful, almost all of the songs break at least one of these rules.

Ultimately I settled on the official soundtrack of the Charlie Brown Christmas Special, by Vince Guaraldi. It's the closest I've found so far to fitting all these rules, and being able to keep my mind slightly occupied.

If anyone knows of a better album or collection of music for this purpose, let me know!
