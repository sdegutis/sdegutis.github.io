Clojure for web apps, Part 1 I believe Clojure is the best language for writing web apps. So I'll try to explain why in a few blog posts. Here's the first one. _Update:_ Also see [Part 2](/blog/2013-11-14-clojure-for-web-apps-p2). ### It's data-oriented A typical web app does a lot of data processing. And we all like expressive, readable code. That's why in the past I'd choose Ruby for this task. It's known for reading like plain English. But take a look at this Clojure snippet: ```clojure (->> orders (remove :paid?) (filter (partial created-during? [(date 2011 01 01) (date 2011 02 01)])) (mapcat :payments) (filter cash-payment?) (map :amount) (reduce +)) ``` It reads like a poem! Even if you don't know any Clojure, you might be able to guess what it does: 1. It takes a collection of orders, 2. filters out ones that aren't paid, 3. finds only ones created during Jan 2011, 4. gets all their payments, 5. finds only cash payments, 6. gets the amounts, 7. and adds them together.
