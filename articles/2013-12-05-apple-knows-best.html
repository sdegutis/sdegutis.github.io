Apple Knows Best I'm not so sure about that anymore. Lately I've been doing things in very non-Apple ways and finding that my productivity is increasing for it. By non-Apple ways, I mean I've been bypassing their own systems and disabling their preferences. Let me give some examples: * I've been mostly using apps not found on the App Store, effectively ignoring their "Mac App Store"-only System Preference restriction. * I've been storing my documents in Dropbox rather than iCloud. * I've written a keyboard-based window manager which I now use almost exclusively to move/resize my windows. * I wrote a music player app and now I use that every day instead of iTunes. * I haven't used the new "tags" feature. Or their "custom searches" feature that I think the "tags" replaced. * I haven't used iCloud or my AppleID-based email address. * I haven't used any app in the iLife suit for years. * I've been disabling the new "sandbox" in every new app I create, and I don't distribute any of them on the Mac App Store. * Most of my apps use APIs that Apple doesn't even allow on the App Store. * I tried and tried and just can't use Apple Mail. * I don't use Time Machine. I'm not entirely sure what to make of this yet. But I sure do know what it _looks like_. It looks like the beginning of a break-up.
