Difficulty Making Progress? Give Yourself Limitations Creativity can be daunting. Restrictions set you free! Don’t believe me? * YouTube had a 10-minute limit on videos. This helped them with writing their video player and scaling their website more predictably. * [Minecraft](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=LnjSWPxJxNs) threw out the idea of 3D-realism, making everything blocky. This simplified _everything_ and allowed Notch to make the game in _months_. * Dwarf Fortress only uses colored text for all of its graphics, and it’s one of the most long-lived games there ever was, and still going strong. * Vine only allowed video clips of up to 6 seconds. [Musical.ly](https://www.reddit.com/r/videos/comments/8vs5na/what_kids_really_do_on_musically/) a similar time limit on videos, with a focus on personal short music videos. * The original Star Wars movies didn’t have CGI, so they had to create and work with physical scale models of spaceships and robots. Because when you limit yourself, you’re avoiding positive infinity and negative infinity, and focusing on the infinity that exists between 0 and 1. Here are some examples, try picking one and see if it helps: 1. See what you can draw in less than 60 minutes. 2. Try drawing or painting something using only 3 colors. 3. Write a short story without using the letter “e” at all. 4. Draw something you like using only big triangles. 5. Compose a story where the first letter of every word tells another story. So if you’re creating something and feeling stuck, try setting limitations for yourself, and hold yourself to them. Give it a try. You may be surprised at just how much more creative you can be, when you limit yourself to become free!
