Good usability My blog's new design was created with high usability in mind. The main principle is for users to be able to find the content very quickly, and one of the chief ways I ensure that is through minimalism and consistency. For example, I don't color my hair green, or wear bright red shoes, or put 30 piercings on my face. There's nothing wrong with doing those things, but it would distract you from _me_. If you wanted to have a conversation with me, you might find that there's more content in my appearance than what I actually have to say. Similarly, I don't put flashy frills on this site. In fact, I make sure that it's very low-key so that the content stands out, rather than the design. While it's true that appearance itself can communicate a message, I would rather my site's (and my) appearance not get in the way of the message it is trying to convey through its actual content. Besides minimalism, consistency is a huge factor. If you notice on this site, I've strived to make content of similar types almost always (nearly) the same font sizes. The whole site uses a single typeface as well. Notice also that the links are all the same color, with the same dashed underline pattern and same highlight effects. The only exception is in the entry-metadata section below each entry, which is slightly lighter in color, in order to give a clear separation between the content and the content's metadata, as well as demarcating entries themselves. I really do hope that this design helps to improve the experience of any visitors or passers-by of this website.
