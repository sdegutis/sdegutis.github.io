How to Program 1. Bridge the gap between your known-knowns and known-unknowns by writing code 2. Turn known-unknowns into knowns by closing the knowledge gap 3. Discover unknown-unknowns along the way, which then become known-unknowns In other words, you want to solve a problem, and you only know part of it. So you start coding the parts that you know how to do. Eventually you'll make the gap smaller between what you don't know how to do and what you do know how to do. That gap will get small enough where you'll suddenly know how to do what you didn't know how to do before. Along the way, you'll run into new things that you need to do but don't know how. As long as you keep following this process of writing the code that you know how to write, you'll eventually finish the whole project, having learned all that you needed to along the way. This isn't entirely true though. Sometimes the gap between what you know and don't know is still too big, and you have to stop and do a little research. But by that time, you'll have a much clearer idea of what to research, so it'll probably be really quick.
