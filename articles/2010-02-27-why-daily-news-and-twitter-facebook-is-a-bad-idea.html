Why daily news (and twitter/facebook) is a bad idea. Events don't happen on a daily basis. Doing cool things can take days, weeks, months, or even decades. "Rome wasn't built in a day," so to speak. So why do we demand news on a daily basis? Why do we expect our twitter and facebook friends to have something interesting to say every few hours? That's not really fair to anyone. Things happen slowly. If I write a brand new awesome framework, it's probably going to take weeks to finish. And during those weeks, I doubt I'll have many interesting things to say about it. Similarly, expecting television/radio news stations and newspaper to have something new and important to say every day kind of defeats the purpose of having news at all. When they run out of actually relevant new information, and when we still expect them to deliver the next day, they have 3 choices: lie and make stuff up, make news happen themselves, or exaggerate (which is a variation of the first). We don't tolerate the first 2 very well, so news reporters either don't do it, or hide it very well. So that only leaves exaggerating. Ever wonder why news anchors use very emotionally-charged language and make a big whoop out of small events? Essentially, we're forcing their hands. We demand something when there is nothing, and complain when the quality decreases. What else do we expect? Seriously. Because of this decline in quality of news, I've stopped watching news years ago, have not touched a newspaper in even longer, and have only visited news sites when they were linked to on twitter (sigh). But this leaves me with a problem... How am I supposed to know if World War 3 has started, or if there's a tornado watch in my area? But for the vast majority of my days, skipping the news entirely works out perfectly and saves me tons of stress.
