Clojure and C I really like Clojure and C. They're both really simple, having very few features that somehow work surprisingly well together. They're both data-oriented languages. Clojure is great at manipulating (sometimes infinite!) sequences. C is great at working with complex custom data structures. They both work out different parts of my brain. I can't see myself ever ditching either. To me, Clojure and C complement each other. My biggest wish for Clojure is a slimmer host environment. The JVM (and the JS-VM and CLR) feel way too heavy-weight for some problems I want to solve in it, like writing command-line utilities. My biggest wish for C is namespaces. Then, a built-in (optional!) notion of reference-counting. And after that, I'd love a cleaned up standard library that makes use of my first two wishes. But other than that, I'm pretty happy with C.
